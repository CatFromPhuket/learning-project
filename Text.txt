List of examples of cross-cultural misunderstandings

In China people pronounced ‘Benz’ in Mercedes - Benz in a different way than European, and in China language it means «Rush to the death».  Certainly, nobody wanted to buy such cars. 	
Gerber (a brand of baby milk) didn't sell well in South America because in Spanish, witch is native for most of South Americans, ‘Gerber’ means to vomit. 
One enthusiastic web-designer  found a picture for a main screen of the website of a product, that they were going to sell in Asia and Arabic countries.  A significant part of this picture was occupied by a feet image. Feet are supposed to be unclean and offensive in Asia and Arabic cultures.
For every human in Europe swastika without any alternative associated with Hitler, Nazism, and horrors of WWII. But for Indians it is just an ancient symbol of fertility.
